Legs Day

Quads · Hamstrings · Glutes · Calves

The biggest session of the week. Lead with hack squat or leg press while your legs are completely fresh, then hammer the hamstrings from two angles. Don't skip calves. Tap "Form guide" on any exercise for cues and common mistakes.

1

Hack squat or leg press

quadsglutes
4 × 8–10Rest 2–3 min

Feet low and narrow on the platform for maximum quad focus. Control the descent — don't let the weight drop. Full depth if your mobility allows.

Setup

  • Feet low and narrow for quad focus
  • Back flat against the pad
  • Unlock safety handles before lowering
  • Toes slightly pointed out — 15–30°

Execution cues

  • Lower slowly — 3 seconds down
  • Aim for thighs parallel or below
  • Drive through the full foot on the way up
  • Don't lock knees fully at the top

Common mistakes

  • Knees caving inward — push them out
  • Lower back peeling off the pad
  • Cutting depth short
  • Locking safeties before completing the set

2

Lying leg curl

hamstrings
4 × 10–12Rest 90 sec

Curl heels to glutes, squeeze at the top, then lower slowly over 3 seconds. The eccentric is where most hamstring growth happens — don't rush it.

Setup

  • Adjust pad so it sits just above the heels
  • Lie face down, hips flat on the bench
  • Hold handles lightly for stability

Execution cues

  • Curl heels toward glutes smoothly
  • Squeeze hard at the top — pause 1 second
  • Lower over 3 seconds — don't let it drop
  • Hips stay flat — don't lift them to assist

Common mistakes

  • Hips rising off the bench to help the curl
  • Dropping the weight quickly on descent
  • Pad positioned too high or too low

3

Leg extension

quads
3 × 12–15Rest 60 sec

Pause and squeeze at the top for 1 second on every rep. Pure quad isolation — you should feel a strong burn by rep 12. Control the weight on the way down.

Setup

  • Adjust seat so knees are at the edge of the seat
  • Pad rests on lower shin, just above ankles
  • Sit upright, grip handles

Execution cues

  • Extend fully — squeeze quads hard at the top
  • Pause for 1 second at full extension
  • Lower slowly — 2–3 seconds
  • Don't let the stack touch between reps

Common mistakes

  • Not reaching full extension at the top
  • Swinging or using momentum
  • Seat adjusted too far back — knee stress

4

Seated leg curl

hamstrings
3 × 12–15Rest 60 sec

The seated position stretches the hamstring at a different angle to the lying curl — pairing both in the same session gives more complete hamstring development.

Setup

  • Adjust back pad so knees sit just off the seat edge
  • Top pad rests on lower quads
  • Feet flexed, toes pointing up

Execution cues

  • Curl as far as the machine allows
  • Squeeze at full contraction
  • Lower slowly — 3 seconds
  • Feel the hamstring stretch on the return

Common mistakes

  • Not curling through full range of motion
  • Lifting hips off the seat during the curl
  • Going too heavy — ruins control

5

Walking lunges

quadsgluteshamstrings
3 × 10/legRest 90 sec

Keep torso upright throughout. Knee tracks over your second toe — don't let it cave inward. Full stride length activates more glute. Use dumbbells to add load.

Setup

  • Stand tall, core braced
  • Dumbbells at sides if adding load
  • Take a long stride forward

Execution cues

  • Keep torso upright — don't lean forward
  • Front knee tracks over second toe
  • Lower back knee toward floor — 1–2 inches off
  • Drive through front heel to step forward

Common mistakes

  • Front knee caving inward
  • Torso leaning too far forward
  • Short stride — limits glute activation
  • Back knee slamming the floor

6

Standing calf raises

calves
4 × 15–20Rest 60 sec

Full range of motion is essential — deep stretch at the bottom, hold the contraction at the top for 1 second. Calves respond to volume, so don't rush through these.

Setup

  • Stand on edge of a step or plate if available
  • Balls of feet on the edge — heels hanging
  • Hold something stable for balance

Execution cues

  • Lower heels below the step level — full stretch
  • Raise up onto tiptoes as high as possible
  • Hold the contraction at the top for 1 second
  • Slow and controlled throughout

Common mistakes

  • Bouncing at the bottom — lose the stretch
  • Not achieving full range — partial reps
  • Going too fast — calves need slow controlled reps
