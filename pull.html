Pull Day

Back · Biceps · Rear Delts

Lead with the chest-supported row while your back is fresh. Face pulls are non-negotiable every session — they keep your shoulders healthy and balanced against all the pressing on Monday.

1

Chest-supported machine row

mid back lats rear delt bicep
4 × 8–10 Rest 2 min

Chest pinned to the pad throughout — this removes lower back fatigue and lets you load the back harder. Pull handles to lower chest and squeeze hard at the end.

Pull-ups or lat pulldown

lats bicep rear delt
4 × 6–10 Rest 2 min

Full range of motion — aim for chest to bar on pull-ups, or pull the bar to your upper chest on lat pulldown. Use the assisted machine if needed and work towards unassisted.

3

Seated cable row

mid back lats bicep
3 × 10–12 Rest 90 sec

Pull to your lower chest, keep your chest up and proud. Retract shoulder blades fully at the end of the movement — don't just pull with your arms.

4

Face pulls

rear delt rotator cuff
3 × 15 Rest 60 sec

Rope to forehead height, externally rotate at the end of the rep — think about pulling your thumbs toward your ears. Do these every single pull session. Your shoulders will thank you.

5

Bicep curl machine

bicep
3 × 10–12 Rest 60 sec

The machine keeps constant tension on the bicep throughout the full range of motion — no cheating or swinging possible. Squeeze hard at the top, then lower slowly over 3 seconds. Great for isolating the bicep without involving the back.

Hammer curls

brachialis bicep
3 × 10–12 Rest 60 sec

Neutral grip (thumbs up). Targets the brachialis which sits underneath the bicep — developing it pushes the bicep peak higher and thickens the arm overall.
