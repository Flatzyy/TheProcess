Pull Day

Back · Biceps · Rear Delts

Lead with the chest-supported row while your back is fresh. Face pulls are non-negotiable every session — they keep your shoulders healthy and balanced. Tap "Form guide" on any exercise for cues and common mistakes.

1

Chest-supported machine row

mid backlatsrear deltbicep
4 × 8–10Rest 2 min

Chest pinned to the pad throughout — this removes lower back fatigue and lets you load the back harder. Pull handles to lower chest and squeeze hard at the end.

Setup

  • Chest pressed firmly against the pad throughout
  • Feet flat on footrests, stable base
  • Arms fully extended before each rep

Execution cues

  • Pull handles to lower chest — not stomach
  • Lead with elbows, drive them back
  • Squeeze shoulder blades together at the end
  • Pause 1 second at contraction, lower slowly

Common mistakes

  • Letting chest come off the pad
  • Pulling to the stomach instead of chest
  • Using biceps only — think elbows not hands
  • Not reaching full extension between reps

2

Lat pulldown

latsbiceprear delt
4 × 8–12Rest 2 min

Pull the bar to your upper chest, slight lean back, and drive your elbows down toward your hips. The lat pulldown will build the strength to progress to pull-ups over time — focus on full range and control.

Setup

  • Grip just wider than shoulder-width
  • Thighs locked under the pad securely
  • Slight lean back — chest up toward the bar
  • Arms fully extended at the start

Execution cues

  • Initiate by pulling shoulder blades down first
  • Drive elbows down toward your hips
  • Pull bar to your upper chest — not behind neck
  • Lower slowly over 2–3 seconds — feel the lat stretch

Common mistakes

  • Pulling behind the neck — neck strain risk
  • Using biceps only — engage the lats
  • Leaning back too far — becomes a row
  • Dropping the weight quickly on the way up

3

Single-arm dumbbell row

mid backlatsbicep
3 × 10–12/sideRest 90 sec

Place one knee and hand on the bench, back flat and parallel to the floor. Row the dumbbell to your hip — not your shoulder. Each side works independently, great for fixing any left-right imbalances.

Setup

  • Same-side knee and hand on the bench for support
  • Back flat and parallel to the floor — don't round
  • Dumbbell hanging straight down from shoulder
  • Neutral grip — palm facing your body

Execution cues

  • Drive elbow back toward your hip — not your shoulder
  • Think about putting your elbow in your back pocket
  • Squeeze the lat and mid back at the top
  • Lower slowly with full control — feel the stretch
  • Keep hips square — don't rotate to help the lift

Common mistakes

  • Rowing to the shoulder — targets traps not back
  • Rotating the torso to lift heavier
  • Rounding the lower back
  • Rushing the eccentric on the way down

4

Face pulls

rear deltrotator cuff
3 × 15Rest 60 sec

Rope to forehead height, externally rotate at the end of the rep — think about pulling your thumbs toward your ears. Do these every single pull session.

Setup

  • Set cable to upper chest or eye level
  • Rope attachment, grip with thumbs pointing back
  • Step back to create tension before starting

Execution cues

  • Pull rope toward your forehead — not your chin
  • Externally rotate at the end — thumbs back toward ears
  • Elbows should finish high and wide
  • Control the return fully

Common mistakes

  • Pulling to the chin — becomes an upright row
  • Skipping the external rotation at the end
  • Going too heavy — ruins the movement
  • Elbows dropping below shoulder height

5

Bicep curl machine

bicep
3 × 10–12Rest 60 sec

The machine keeps constant tension on the bicep throughout. Squeeze hard at the top, lower slowly over 3 seconds. Great for isolating the bicep without involving the back.

Setup

  • Adjust seat so elbows align with the machine pivot point
  • Upper arms flat on the pad throughout
  • Grip handles with palms facing up

Execution cues

  • Curl to full contraction — squeeze at the top
  • Lower slowly over 3 seconds
  • Don't let the weight stack touch between reps
  • Keep upper arms pinned to the pad

Common mistakes

  • Lifting upper arms off the pad mid-rep
  • Dropping the weight quickly on the way down
  • Not adjusting seat height — misaligned pivot

6

Hammer curls

brachialisbicep
3 × 10–12Rest 60 sec

Neutral grip — thumbs pointing forward. Targets the brachialis which sits underneath the bicep, pushing the peak higher and thickening the arm overall.

Setup

  • Stand upright, dumbbells at sides
  • Neutral grip — thumbs pointing forward
  • Elbows pinned to sides

Execution cues

  • Curl straight up — don't rotate the wrist
  • Squeeze at the top of the movement
  • Lower slowly — 2–3 seconds
  • Alternate arms or do both together

Common mistakes

  • Swinging the body to lift the weight
  • Rotating the wrist — becomes a regular curl
  • Elbows drifting forward away from the body
