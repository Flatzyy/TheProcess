Push Day

Chest · Shoulders · Triceps

Start with the two heavy compound movements while you're fresh, then move into isolation work. Rest times matter — use them fully on the big lifts. Tap "Form guide" on any exercise to see setup cues and common mistakes.

1

Barbell bench press

chestfront delttricep
4 × 6–8Rest 2–3 min

Retract your shoulder blades and drive your feet into the floor. Lower the bar to your lower chest, touch lightly, and press up and back. Your primary compound lift — load it progressively every week.

Setup

  • Feet flat on floor, drive them into the ground
  • Retract and depress shoulder blades — create a stable base
  • Slight arch in lower back, chest up
  • Grip just wider than shoulder-width

Execution cues

  • Lower bar to lower chest in a slight diagonal path
  • Lightly touch chest — don't bounce
  • Drive feet into floor as you press up
  • Press bar back and slightly toward your face
  • Lock out fully at the top

Common mistakes

  • Elbows flaring to 90° — keep at 45°
  • Bouncing the bar off the chest
  • Feet coming off the floor mid-rep
  • Losing shoulder blade retraction under load

2

Incline dumbbell press

upper chestfront delt
3 × 8–10Rest 90 sec

Set bench to 30–45°. Keep elbows at 45° from your torso — this protects the shoulder and keeps tension on the upper chest.

Setup

  • Set bench to 30–45° — higher angles shift load to shoulders
  • Sit back into the pad, feet flat
  • Dumbbells at shoulder height, palms forward
  • Retract shoulder blades before pressing

Execution cues

  • Keep elbows at 45° from torso throughout
  • Lower slowly until dumbbells are level with chest
  • Press up and slightly together at the top
  • Don't fully lock elbows — keep slight bend

Common mistakes

  • Bench too steep — becomes a shoulder press
  • Elbows flaring to 90° — shoulder strain risk
  • Rushing the descent
  • Not reaching full range at the bottom

3

Arnold press

all 3 delt headstricep
4 × 8–10Rest 2 min

Start with palms facing you, rotate outward as you press overhead. The rotation hits all three shoulder heads in one movement.

Setup

  • Sit upright, back supported, core braced
  • Start with palms facing you at chin height
  • Elbows roughly at 90°

Execution cues

  • Rotate palms outward as you press overhead
  • Palms fully facing forward at the top
  • Reverse the rotation on the way down
  • Control the tempo — don't rush the rotation

Common mistakes

  • Skipping the rotation — becomes a standard press
  • Leaning back excessively under load
  • Letting the core go slack at the top

4

Lateral raises

side delt
3 × 12–15Rest 60 sec

Slight forward lean, lead with your elbows not your hands. Control the descent slowly — 2–3 seconds down for maximum delt stimulus.

Setup

  • Stand with slight forward lean at the hips
  • Slight bend in elbows throughout
  • Palms facing down, dumbbells at sides

Execution cues

  • Lead with elbows, not hands
  • Raise to just above shoulder height
  • Think about pouring water from a jug at the top
  • Lower over 2–3 seconds — don't drop

Common mistakes

  • Using momentum to swing the weights up
  • Shrugging the traps at the top
  • Going too heavy — ruins the movement
  • Dropping the weight on the way down

5

Cable fly or pec deck

chest
3 × 12–15Rest 60 sec

Keep a slight bend in elbows throughout. Squeeze hard at the point of peak contraction — pause for 1 second. Pure chest isolation.

Setup

  • Set cables at chest height or sit upright (pec deck)
  • Slight fixed bend in elbows — don't change this
  • Chest up, shoulder blades back

Execution cues

  • Think about hugging a tree — arc the arms
  • Squeeze hard at the point of peak contraction
  • Pause for 1 second at the peak
  • Control the return — feel the stretch

Common mistakes

  • Bending and straightening elbows — not a press
  • Going too heavy — ruins the arc motion
  • Not pausing at contraction

6

Tricep rope pushdown

tricep
3 × 12–15Rest 60 sec

Elbows pinned to sides throughout. Spread the rope apart at the bottom of the movement and fully extend. Don't let elbows flare.

Setup

  • Set cable to high position
  • Stand close to the cable, slight forward lean
  • Elbows pinned to sides — they don't move

Execution cues

  • Push down and spread the rope apart at the bottom
  • Fully extend the arms at the bottom
  • Control the return to about 90°
  • Only the forearms should move

Common mistakes

  • Elbows flaring out during the rep
  • Using bodyweight to push down
  • Not fully extending at the bottom

7

Overhead tricep extension

tricep long head
3 × 10–12Rest 60 sec

Keep elbows pointing forward, lower the dumbbell slowly behind your head. The stretch on the long head is key — don't rush the eccentric.

Setup

  • Sit or stand with core braced
  • Hold dumbbell overhead with both hands
  • Elbows pointing forward — not flared

Execution cues

  • Lower dumbbell behind head slowly — 3 seconds
  • Feel the stretch in the tricep long head
  • Press back up to full extension overhead
  • Keep elbows pointing forward throughout

Common mistakes

  • Elbows flaring wide — loses long head tension
  • Rushing the eccentric — the stretch is the point
  • Arching the lower back excessively
