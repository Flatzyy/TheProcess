Key rules

What to remember across every phase

Protein first, always

Hit your protein target before anything else. Once protein is covered, fill remaining calories however you like. Protein is the one macro you should never compromise on.

Calories matter most

Total calorie intake is the primary driver of fat loss and muscle gain. A slight deficit loses fat, a slight surplus builds muscle. Hit the right number for each phase.

Carbs around training

On gym days, keep carbs around your session for fuel and recovery. On rest days, reduce carbs slightly — your body doesn't need as much glycogen when it's not training.

Don't go below 50g fat

Fat is essential for hormone production and recovery. No matter which phase you're in, never drop fats below 50g per day — it will negatively affect energy and progress.

Creatine every day

5g creatine monohydrate daily — stir into overnight oats or your protein shake. Take it consistently including rest days. One of the only supplements with solid evidence behind it.

Reassess every 4 weeks

Weigh yourself at the same time each week. If fat loss stalls for 3 weeks, reduce calories by 100–150 kcal via carbs. If weight gain exceeds 0.5kg/week in the bulk, pull back slightly.
